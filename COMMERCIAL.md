## What counts as commercial use

Per the PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0 definitions:

- **Non-commercial (no separate license needed)**:
  - Personal projects, hobby, study, religious observance
  - Academic research at universities, government labs, public research orgs
  - Educational use in classrooms / teaching
  - Charitable / public-interest organizations
  - Reading, learning from, copying, modifying the code privately
  - Publishing academic papers describing the work (with citation)
  - Open-source non-commercial forks

- **Commercial (separate license needed)**:
  - Building a product or service that you charge for
  - Using Atlas-generated visuals in commercial publications, ads, packaging
  - Hosting an Atlas-based generator service for paying users
  - Building proprietary tooling on top of Atlas for internal company use
  - Selling preset libraries, renderer plugins, or scene packs that depend on Atlas
  - Reselling, sublicensing, or relicensing the Atlas Project code

If you're unsure whether your use is commercial, contact us — we'd
rather have a 5-minute clarifying conversation than discover later.

## How to get a commercial license

Email us:

- **Primary contact**: stormspire100@gmail.com
- **Subject prefix**: `[Atlas Commercial License]`

In your email, tell us:

1. **Who you are** — individual / company name, role, location
2. **What you want to build** — brief description of the commercial product or service
3. **How you plan to use Atlas** — which components (renderer / scene engine /
   Compositor / etc.), and whether you plan to modify
4. **Estimated scale** — users / scenes generated / revenue / etc.

We'll respond within a few business days with a license proposal sized
to your use case. License terms are typically annual fees or revenue
share, scaled to your business model.

## Why PolyForm Noncommercial?

Atlas is a generative-art engine designed for commercial deployment as
a hosted service plus marketplace for renderer/scene/preset assets.
The commercial license ensures we can sustain the work; the
non-commercial license ensures the broad creative-coding and research
community can use it freely.

This is a common pattern (Sentry, Plausible, and many other source-available
projects use the same license structure). If the model doesn't work for
your specific case, please email us — we're willing to negotiate
non-standard terms for unusual circumstances.
