Notes on AI Apps in 2026

Anish Acharya argues AI applications are maturing along several lines. Tools must shift from execution-focused (IDEs, Figma) to exploration-focused platforms for ideation. Every organizational function — marketing, legal, finance, HR — should become software-first, with Codex and Claude Code enabling non-technical teams. Specialized AI apps will diverge from foundation models, combining multi-model orchestration with domain-specific interfaces; in coding alone, startups generated over $1 billion of new revenue in 2025. Consumers are discovering AI beyond chatbots through platforms like Wabi and ChatGPT's image tools. If Tesla delivers coast-to-coast Full Self-Driving and Claude Code can write Claude Code, current AI capabilities already serve most enterprise tasks.
