Investing Capital to Defend the Nation

David Ulevitch argues capital investment in American industrial capacity is a national security imperative. The U.S. led in 60 of 64 critical technologies in the early 2000s but now leads in only seven, while China leads in 57. He proposes reframing investment strategy from ESG to Global Competition and Innovation (GCI), identifying eight critical sectors including semiconductors, energy, AI, biotech, and space. Large institutional investors — pension funds, sovereign wealth vehicles, insurance companies, and endowments — must anchor capital in manufacturing, AI deployment, and energy scaling. This is financially rational fiduciary duty, not charity.
