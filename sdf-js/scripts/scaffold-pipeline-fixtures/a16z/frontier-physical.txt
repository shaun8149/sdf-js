Frontier Systems for the Physical World

Oliver Hsu identifies five technical primitives enabling AI in physical domains: learned representations of physical dynamics, embodied action architectures, simulation infrastructure, expanded sensory capabilities, and closed-loop agentic systems. These support three frontier areas: robotics, autonomous science, and novel human-computer interfaces including BCIs and wearables. VLAs, WAMs, and native embodied models like Generalist's GEN-1 advance physics understanding. RL post-training via RECAP enables systems like pi-0.6 to fold laundry across 50 garment types. Self-driving laboratories from Periodic Labs and Medra combine scientific reasoning with physical testing. The domains reinforce each other, creating a flywheel for scaling AI into the physical realm.
