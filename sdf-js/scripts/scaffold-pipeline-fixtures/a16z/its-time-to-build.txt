It's Time to Build

Marc Andreessen's April 2020 essay argues Western institutions failed during the coronavirus pandemic due to inability to build: insufficient tests, ventilators, ICU beds, masks, and gowns, with New York City requesting rain ponchos as protective equipment. The problem is not money — Congress passed a $2 trillion relief package in two weeks — but desire, regulatory capture, and political will. He critiques housing prices in San Francisco, education capacity serving a microscopic share of 4 million annual U.S. eighteen-year-olds and 120 million globally, offshored manufacturing, and absent infrastructure like supersonic aircraft, delivery drones, and high-speed trains. Both political sides should prioritize building.
