Redo Announces $81 Million Series B to Support Commerce Technology Expansion

Draper, Utah, June 23, 2026 — Redo, the commerce technology platform, today announced an $81 million Series B financing at a $1.25 billion valuation. The round was led by Smash Capital, with participation from existing investors Pelion Venture Partners and Cervin Ventures.

More than 4,100 brands use the Redo platform today, and over 1,750 merchants use multiple Redo offerings across the suite.

"Every merchant is fighting to own the relationship with their shoppers," said Sterling Snow, co-founder and CEO of Redo. "This funding allows us to accelerate that mission and build the infrastructure brands need."

"Redo started by building trust with merchants through post purchase experiences and has expanded into a much larger opportunity," said Paul Szurek of Smash Capital, which led the round.

The company said the new capital will fund development of AI-driven commerce tools, including agentic shopping experiences tailored to individual shoppers, a marketing agent for personalized email and text campaigns, and a post-purchase concierge service designed to increase customer lifetime value.

"Our network allows customers to return products locally, where we can inspect, refurbish, re-fulfill, and reship inventory," said Aaron Evett, chief commerce officer.

Alongside the financing, Redo announced the acquisition of ReturnBear, extending returns servicing coverage to more than 100 countries, and outlined plans for international expansion of its merchant network.
